The Palestinian Cause

The Palestinian cause is one of the longest and most complex issues of the modern era. It began with the occupation of Palestine in 1948 and the establishment of the state of Israel on its land, which led to the displacement of hundreds of thousands of Palestinians in what became known as the Nakba. The essence of the issue lies in the right of the Palestinian people to self-determination, the establishment of their independent state on their land with East Jerusalem as its capital, in addition to the right of refugees to return to their homes.

The issue is not merely a border dispute, but rather a matter of homeland, identity, and dignity for a people striving for freedom. It includes political, humanitarian, and religious dimensions due to the status of Jerusalem and Al-Aqsa Mosque. Despite the ongoing sacrifices and suffering, Palestinians remain steadfast in their right to the land, return, and living in a just peace that guarantees them freedom and independence.

The Tragedy of Gaza Since October 2023

Since October 7, 2023, Palestine has been living through one of the harshest tragedies in its modern history, as Gaza has turned into a city of rubble under bombardment and siege. Thousands of martyrs, most of them children and women, have departed in painful silence, while mothers continue to bid farewell to their children with tears and broken hearts. Hospitals and schools have not been spared from destruction, and survivors live without medicine or food, besieged by hunger and fear. Gaza is bleeding, and every Palestinian home carries an unhealed wound, while Palestine's cry still rises: Is there a conscience that hears the call?
